// CASE STUDIES

Deep Dives That Delivered

Three examples of taking a problem from unclear symptom to measurable resolution — with full ownership.

● Platform Stability

Cutting Multi-Device Session Failures from 6% to 2.3%

The Problem

The Multi-Device Testing (MDT) feature had a 6% error rate — meaning 1 in 17 sessions was failing. Users experiencing failures were churning, and the team didn't have clear visibility into why sessions broke.

Why It Mattered

MDT was a flagship feature tied directly to paid plan upgrades and enterprise deals. High failure rates were silently eroding trust and revenue.

What I Did

Systematically categorized every failure mode into buckets and identified the dominant root cause. Deployed targeted fixes at the protocol level, cleaned up resource leaks causing ghost sessions, and deep-dived into reconnection edge cases. Built a new Stability Tracking Dashboard so the team could monitor regression trends in real time going forward.

6%→2.3%
Live MDT error rate
4.8%→3.3%
AppLive error rate
~40%
Drop in single session bad metrics
● Feature Adoption

Growing MDT Repeat Usage from Under 10% to 29%

The Problem

Users were discovering Multi-Device Testing but not coming back. Repeat usage was below 10%, suggesting the feature wasn't sticky enough to change user behavior long-term.

Why It Mattered

One-time feature usage doesn't convert to plan upgrades. Repeat usage drives retention and ultimately revenue. The product team needed experiments that would change the habit loop.

What I Did

Designed and executed the Add Device CTA experiment across three user cohorts (FTUs, Paid Non-Pro, Paid Pro) on both Live and AppLive. Added a silhouette reminder that persisted after dismissal. Coordinated all FE and BE PRs across teams, personally secured a paid account for production validation. Ran the entire experiment lifecycle end-to-end.

● Automation & Ops

Reducing Incident Response Time from 5 Minutes to Under 1 Minute

The Problem

Critical production alerts required manual diagnosis across multiple data sources. When something broke in the middle of the night, the on-call engineer had to pull logs, query dashboards, and piece together context manually — taking 5+ minutes just to understand what was happening.

Why It Mattered

Every extra minute of diagnostic delay is a minute the product is down for real users. For a testing platform where developers pay to stay productive, downtime has a direct revenue cost.

What I Did

Built automated Tripwire diagnostics that aggregate signals from multiple sources and surface a structured diagnosis to the Ops engineer within seconds of an alert firing. Also created comprehensive runbooks and dashboards so any engineer — even newly onboarded — could act immediately. Ops onboarding time for new engineers dropped significantly.
